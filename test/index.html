GFSS – ElectroMagnetic Gravity Separation (EMGS)

Advanced mineral processing technology that combines gravity, magnetic, and electrostatic forces for high-efficiency, chemical-free separation with reduced environmental impact.

Introduction

GFSS integrates gravity separation with electromagnetic and electrostatic fields to exploit differences in density, magnetic susceptibility, and electrical conductivity. This results in superior recovery rates while minimizing water and energy use — a key technology for sustainable mining and recycling.

1. Feed System

Crushed ore or material slurry is introduced into the separator.

2. Gravity Module

Particles are stratified by density using centrifugal or settling forces.

3. Electromagnetic Module

High-intensity magnetic fields separate ferromagnetic and paramagnetic particles.

4. Electrostatic Module

Electric fields differentiate particles based on conductivity.

5. Collection System

Output is divided into concentrates, middlings, and tailings.

Applications

• Rare earth and critical mineral extraction
• Iron ore, gold, and base metal beneficiation
• E-waste and metal recycling
• Advanced water purification in arid regions
• Future concepts for space material separation
