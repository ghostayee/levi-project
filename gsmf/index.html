GSMF – Gas Separation Membrane Filtration

High-efficiency, low-energy membrane technology for clean gas separation, carbon capture, and sustainable industrial processes.

Introduction

GSMF uses selective polymer or ceramic membranes to separate gases by molecular size, polarity, and solubility. It efficiently separates key industrial gases such as CO₂, CH₄, H₂, N₂, and O₂ with minimal energy input and without heavy chemicals.

As part of the JWT / PCRR GREEN project, GSMF supports decarbonization, biogas upgrading, hydrogen production, and net-zero goals under UN SDGs and PNRR Mission 2.

Core Technology

Membrane Materials

Polymeric membranes (flexible, cost-effective), ceramic membranes (high-temperature resistant), and mixed-matrix membranes (enhanced selectivity).

Separation Principle

Pressure-driven selective permeability: different gases diffuse through the membrane at different rates based on molecular size and solubility.

Module Design

Hollow fiber bundles, spiral-wound cartridges, or plate-and-frame modules — compact and easy to scale.

Workflow of a GSMF Plant

1. Gas Conditioning

Remove particulates, moisture, and contaminants from the feed gas.

2. Compression

Pressurize the gas to optimal operating levels.

3. Membrane Separation

Gas mixture passes through selective membranes.

4. Collection

Permeate (target gas) and Retentate (remaining gas) are collected separately.
